# Retained session case study

Use this example to understand the method, not as a universal balance target.

## Observation

The real Godot campaign covered six personas and three fixed seeds: 18 cells
and 342 gameplay weeks. Decision validity was 100%, with zero fallback and
provider errors. All 18 cells entered the cashflow/stress attractor, including
personas with unlike intent. Exact facts were frozen in the public campaign
bundle and reviewed before source inspection.

The gameplay rows are real-Godot-derived, while the committed Judge provider
is hash-pinned prerecorded Replay. No live OpenAI persona campaign was run in
this session. That distinction is part of the evidence, not a missing label to
hide; live persona workers remain a supported test strategy requiring separate
provenance.

## Hypothesis and change

Codex inferred that recurring living-cost drift depleted spendable cash and
fed a stress loop faster than persona strategies could recover. It froze fixed
seeds 42–44, unseen holdouts 1042–1044, thresholds, one mechanism class, two
allowed files, and an 80-line maximum. In an isolated game worktree it changed
two files (+35/−5) to smooth the blocked-account allowance interaction. The
focused Godot economy validator passed.

## Proof and decision

Patched mean final cash improved from 0 to about 18 on fixed seeds and from 0
to about 61 on holdouts. Target membership remained 18/18 in every baseline
and patched cohort; maximum stress remained 100. Fixed and holdout target
reduction were both 0%, so the required causal gates failed. Codex rejected the
candidate and did not merge it.

## Transferable lesson

Focused tests proved that the change was legal, and a secondary metric proved
that the parameter moved. Neither proved the player-level failure was fixed.
The fixed/holdout experiment prevented symptom improvement from being
misreported as causal repair. The next experiment must target another link in
the stress/cashflow feedback chain, not widen the same patch or weaken gates.
